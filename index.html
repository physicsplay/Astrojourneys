Playable physics, framed by Big Ideas in Astronomy.

Mission ATLAS is a growing collection of browser-based physics games designed for classroom use. Each mission turns a common misconception into a decision problem: students must calculate, predict, test, and revise. The current set covers lunar mechanics, orbital motion, Mars surface and landing physics, spacecraft electrical systems, planetary defence, quantum optics, and wave-based exploration.

Game 01

🌙 Lunar Rescue

MoonLanding

Guide an emergency cargo capsule to a lunar outpost, managing thrust, attitude, descent speed, and fuel under lunar gravity with no atmospheric drag.

Physics focusNewtonian mechanics, thrust components, lunar gravity, fuel consumption, work–energy, delta-v.
Misconception target"There is almost no gravity on the Moon" and "if the engine is off, motion stops."
Big Ideas in Astronomy7.7 Earth–Moon system · 4.2 Gravitation dominates on large scales.

Game 06

☄️ Asteroid Deflection

New
ImpulsePlanetary defence

Plan a kinetic impactor mission to deflect an asteroid. Choose impactor mass, velocity, and timing — a small early nudge beats a dramatic late strike.

Physics focusMomentum, impulse, Δv, mass estimation (density × volume), lead-time amplification, miss-distance models.
Misconception target"A single massive last-second impact is always best." In reality, a small early deflection gives a much larger miss distance.
Big Ideas in Astronomy11.2 Near-Earth objects are monitored · 7.8 Asteroids are remnants of Solar System formation.

Game 07

🔬 Quantum Rescue: The Photon Lock

New
QuantumOptics

Survive a solar flare emergency by solving three physics puzzles: identify a blackbody spectrum peak, unlock a photoelectric lock, and align an electron beam through a nano-slit.

Physics focusWien's law, Planck spectrum, photoelectric effect (E = hf, Kmax = hf − φ), single-slit diffraction, wave–particle duality.
Misconception target"Brighter light always ejects faster electrons." Frequency determines energy per photon; intensity determines how many.
Big Ideas in Astronomy4.5 Light carries information about its source · 5.8 Spacecraft use technology to study the universe.

Game 08

🌋 Lunar Lava Tube Scout

New
WavesRadar

Scout a lunar lava tube with multi-frequency radar. Trade off penetration depth against resolution, interpret return signatures, and map voids for future habitation.

Physics focusWave reflection, frequency–wavelength–penetration trade-offs, signal interpretation, indirect measurement (d = v·t/2).
Misconception target"Higher frequency is always better." Lower f penetrates deeper; higher f gives finer detail but shallower reach.
