⚠️ Misconception to break

"Electrons are tiny balls — waves need crowds." No: fired one at a time, each electron still builds an interference pattern. Every particle carries a wavelength.

🧪 Model & assumptions (stated honestly)

Non-relativistic electrons (error <0.5% up to 10 keV here) · teaching constant 1.227 nm·eV½ (exact 1.2264) · K[eV] = gun voltage[V] numerically · the on-screen fringe pattern is qualitative (spacing ∝ λ, never graded) · single-electron buildup is real physics — Tonomura, 1989.
