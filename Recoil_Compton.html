⚠️ Misconception to break

"Waves can\u2019t push, and scattered light keeps its wavelength." Classical physics agreed — and was wrong. The photon recoils off an electron like a billiard ball, paying momentum and leaving longer.

🧪 Model & assumptions (stated honestly)

Free, initially-at-rest electron · teaching value λc ≈ 2.43 pm (exact 2.4263) · wavelengths drawn ~10¹¹× larger for viewing (real: picometres) · the electron\u2019s recoil direction is illustrative — its exact formula is another law and stays out of this game · angles are exact.

Difficulty generous tolerances

📐 Key formulas (given)

Compton: Δλ = λc (1 − cos θ), λc = 2.43 pm scattered: λ′ = λ + Δλ (always LONGER) inverse: cos θ = 1 − Δλ/2.43 useful: cos60°=½ · cos90°=0 · cos120°=−½ · cos180°=−1 ceiling: Δλmax = 2λc = 4.86 pm (at 180°)
